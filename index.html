Slaying the Salmon of Doubt

(...and introducing the lobster of uncertainty)

 

A story about how a team of enterprise developers worked their way upstream. Told first at OpenStack Paris 2014 on the How To Contribute track.

Introductions

(...and who is the Salmon of Doubt?)

  • Compute service focus.
  • 12 people. Which is big.
  • Product engineering team. Not a pure upstream team. Has product development and support responsibilities.
  • HP Helion OpenStack, HP Helion Public Cloud, HP Helion OpenStack Community Edition.
  • 2012 to present day.
  • The Salmon of Doubt is: that feeling that you won't be able to contribute upstream and don't know where to start; ...

The Fork

(...and the knife.)

  • We forked openstack.
  • Diablo; enormous pressure to get something in the marketplace; no upstream people.
  • Grizzly: upstream was stable enough and we wanted to catch up. We couldn't.
  • New project to build grizzly based service; tooling to make keeping up with upstream possible; investment in upstream activity.
  • Don't do it.
  • It took us a further 9 months to get a service generally available to the public due.
  • Went live November 2013 and was regularly 1-2 weeks behind trunk for many services.

Silos

(...and an aside on the importance of documentation.)

There are n=3 people on the team, Alice, Bob and Quentin. Quentin asks a question. There are 5 ways he can get an answer:

  1. Q: by finding it himself.
  2. AQ: by asking Alice who tells him immediately.
  3. BQ: by asking Bob who tells him immediately.
  4. BAQ: by asking Alice who has to ask Bob.
  5. ABQ: by asking Bob who has to ask Alice.

Interrupt Driven Development

(...and the emergence of a sentry)

Initially

  • Engineers have multiple (> 5) tasks on the go.
  • Support work is handled by one individual.
  • Dev/Test environments frequently down.

Emerging

  • Daily rotation of “Sentry Duty”.
  • Takes care of all interruptions.
  • Sentry looks after issues in production, triaging incoming bugs, issues with build tooling and dev/test environments.
  • If there's any time left over they will work in improvements, not main task.
“My goal for the next six months is to commit as much code upstream as I would have committed in an afternoon in my last job.”
